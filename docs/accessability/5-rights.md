# Accessibility Rights

In the UK, many organisations are required to make their websites accessible.

This helps ensure that everyone can access online services.

---

## Why Accessibility Matters

Accessible websites help people who:

- use screen readers
- need larger text
- rely on keyboard navigation
- have other accessibility needs

---

## UK Accessibility Regulations

Public sector websites must follow accessibility standards.

This includes many:

- government websites
- NHS services
- local councils
- public organisations

These websites must publish an **accessibility statement** explaining how accessible their services are.

---

## Reporting Accessibility Problems

If you cannot use a website because of accessibility problems, you can report it.

Most public websites include instructions for reporting accessibility issues.

---

:::tip Did you know?
Accessible design benefits everyone, not just people with disabilities.
:::

---

## Key Takeaway

Accessibility rules help ensure that everyone can use important online services.
