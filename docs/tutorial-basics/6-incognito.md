# Incognito and Private Browsing

## What Is Incognito Mode?

Most browsers offer a feature called **Private Browsing**.

It may have different names depending on the browser:

- **Incognito Mode** (Google Chrome)
- **InPrivate Mode** (Microsoft Edge)
- **Private Browsing** (Safari)
- **Private Window** (Firefox)

Private browsing allows you to open a browsing window that **does not save your activity on the device after you close it**.

---

## What Private Browsing Does

When you use private browsing:

- Your **browsing history is not saved**
- **Cookies are deleted** when you close the window
- Websites do **not keep you logged in after the session ends**
- Other people using the same device will **not see what websites you visited**

This can be useful when using a **shared computer**.

---

## When Is It Useful?

Private browsing can be helpful when:

- Using someone else's computer
- Logging into an account on a shared device
- Searching for something you do not want saved in your history
- Testing how a website looks when you are not logged in

---

## What Private Browsing Does NOT Do

Private browsing is often misunderstood.

It **does not make you invisible online**.

It does **not hide your activity from**:

- Websites you visit
- Your internet provider
- Your workplace or school network (if you are using one)

Private browsing simply prevents your **device from saving the browsing history**.

---

:::tip Did you know?
Private browsing mainly protects your **privacy from other people using the same device**.

It does **not stop websites from collecting information** about visits.
:::

---

## How to Open a Private Window

Most browsers allow you to open a private window quickly.

### Using the Menu

1. Open your browser.
2. Click the **menu button** (usually three dots or three lines).
3. Choose **New Incognito Window** or **New Private Window**.


---

## How to Recognise a Private Window

Private windows usually look slightly different.

You might see:

- A darker browser window
- A special icon (often a hat and glasses)
- A message explaining that private browsing is active

---

## Try This

1. Open your browser menu.
2. Select **New Incognito Window** or **New Private Window**.
3. Visit a website.
4. Close the private window.

Now open your **browser history** and notice that the website you visited is not listed.

---

## Common Confusion

• Incognito mode does **not hide you from websites**.  
• It does **not protect you from scams or viruses**.  
• It simply stops the browser from saving your activity on the device.

---

## Key Takeaway

Private browsing prevents your **device from saving your browsing history**, but it does **not make you anonymous online**.
