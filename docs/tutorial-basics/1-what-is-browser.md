# What Is a Browser?


A **browser** is the program you use to visit websites on the internet. Devices like laptop, phone, tablet have it already installed.  


Think of it like this:

- The **internet** is the road network.
- A **browser** is the car.
- A **website** is the place you travel to.

Without a browser, you cannot view websites. 
---

### Common Browsers

Here are some of the most common browsers:
- Google Chrome  
Made by Google. Very popular and works on most devices.

- Microsoft Edge  
Comes built into most Windows computers.

- Safari  
Built into Apple devices like iPhones, iPads, and Macs.

- Mozilla Firefox  
An independent browser focused strongly on privacy.

### All of these browsers let you:
- Visit websites
- Search for information
- Watch videos
- Shop online
- Check email

They may look slightly different, but they all do the same basic job.

---

## How Do I Know Which Browser I Am Using?

Look for the icon you click to go online.

You might see:
- A colorful circle (Chrome)
- A blue and green wave (Edge)
- A compass (Safari)
- An orange fox around a globe (Firefox)

## Common Confusion

• Google is not the internet but a search engine website  
• A browser is not the same as Wi-Fi.  
• You can use Google search inside different browsers.

---

:::tip Did You Know?

About **70% of people in the world use Google Chrome** as their main web browser.

Because Chrome is so widely used, many websites are designed and tested first using Chrome.

This means that sometimes:
- A website may look slightly different in another browser
- Some features might work better in Chrome
- Occasionally a page may not work fully in older browsers

This does **not mean other browsers are bad**.

Browsers like **Safari, Edge, and Firefox** are all modern, secure, and widely used. Most websites work perfectly well in any of them.
:::
