# 1. What Is a Browser?


A **browser** is the program you use to visit websites on the internet.

Think of it like this:

- The **internet** is the road network.
- A **browser** is the car.
- A **website** is the place you travel to.

Without a browser, you cannot view websites.

---

### Common Browsers

Here are some of the most common browsers:
- Google Chrome
Made by Google. Very popular and works on most devices.

- Microsoft Edge
Comes built into most Windows computers.

- Safari
Built into Apple devices like iPhones, iPads, and Macs.

- Mozilla Firefox
An independent browser focused strongly on privacy.

### All of these browsers let you:
- Visit websites
- Search for information
- Watch videos
- Shop online
- Check email

They may look slightly different, but they all do the same basic job.

---

## How Do I Know Which Browser I Am Using?

Look for the icon you click to go online.

You might see:
- A colourful circle (Chrome)
- A blue and green wave (Edge)
- A compass (Safari)
- An orange fox around a globe (Firefox)

If you are unsure, ask someone to show you once — after that, you’ll recognise it easily.

---

## Why Understanding This Matters

Many people say:

> "I use Google."

What they usually mean is:

- They are using a browser (like Chrome),
- And inside it, they are using Google to search.

Understanding the difference helps you:
- Feel more confident
- Follow instructions more easily
- Solve small problems yourself

---

## Common Confusion

• Google is not the internet.  
• A browser is not the same as Wi-Fi.  
• You can use Google search inside different browsers.

---

## Key Takeaway

A **browser** is simply the tool you use to access and view websites on the internet.
