# What Is Online Tracking?

## What Does "Tracking" Mean?

Online **tracking** means collecting information about how people use websites.

Websites may record things such as:

- Which pages you visit
- What links you click
- How long you stay on a page
- What device you are using

This information is usually collected automatically by computer systems.

---

## Why Do Websites Track Activity?

Tracking is often used for three main reasons.

### Improving Websites

Website owners look at how visitors use their site so they can improve it.

For example, they may check:

- Which pages are most popular
- Where people get stuck
- Which links people click most often

---

### Personalized Advertising

Tracking can also be used to show advertisements based on interests.

For example:

If you search for **walking boots**, you might later see adverts for outdoor clothing or hiking equipment.

This happens because advertising systems try to guess what you may be interested in.

---

### Remembering Preferences

Some tracking helps websites remember useful information such as:

- Your language
- Your location
- Items you recently viewed

This can make websites easier to use.

---

:::tip Did you know?
Most online tracking is done automatically by computer systems analyzing large amounts of data.

It does **not mean someone is personally watching what you do online**.
:::

---

## How Cookies Are Involved

Cookies are one way websites keep track of visits.

A cookie might store information such as:

- When you visited a site
- What pages you viewed
- A unique ID used by the website

This allows websites to recognize returning visitors.

---

## Tracking Across Multiple Websites

Some companies provide advertising services to many different websites.

If several websites use the same advertising service, it can sometimes track visits across those sites.

This helps advertising systems understand **general interests**, such as travel, gardening, or technology.

---

## Can You Reduce Tracking?

Yes. There are several ways to reduce online tracking:

- Adjust cookie settings on websites
- Reject non-essential cookies
- Use private browsing when appropriate
- Change privacy settings in your browser

Later sessions will explore these options in more detail.

:::tip Did you know?
You can reduce tracking by blocking third-party cookies in your browser settings (Menu → Settings → Privacy → Block third-party cookies).
:::
---

## Common Confusion

• Tracking usually studies **patterns across many users**, not individuals.  
• Tracking does **not give websites access to your files or camera**.  
• Tracking mainly helps with advertising and website improvement.

---

## Key Takeaway

Online tracking collects information about how websites are used, often to improve services or show relevant advertisements.
