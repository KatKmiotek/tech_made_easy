# The Address Bar

## What Is the Address Bar?

The **address bar** is the long box at the top of your browser window.

It is where you can:

- Type a website address (for example: bbc.co.uk)
- Type a question (for example: weather in Arrochar)
- Paste a link someone has sent you

It is one of the most important parts of your browser.

---

## What Does It Look Like?

It is usually:

- A long white box at the very top
- Next to the back and forward buttons
- Sometimes shows a small padlock symbol on secure websites

No matter which browser you use, it is always near the top of the screen.

---

## Website Address vs Search

You can use the address bar in two ways.

### 1. Typing a Website Address

Example:

bbc.co.uk  
nhs.uk  
amazon.co.uk  

When you type a full website address, your browser takes you directly there.

---

### 2. Typing a Question

You can also type a question like:

- Bus times from Arrochar to Helensburgh  
- Easy soup recipe  
- How to renew passport  

Your browser will show search results.

Most browsers use a search engine automatically (often Google or Bing). We will look shortly where this is configured.

---

## Important: Google Is Not the Internet

Many people say:

> "I went on Google."

What usually happened is:

- You opened your browser.
- You typed something into the address bar.
- The browser used Google to search.

Google is a **search engine**.
A browser is the **tool** that lets you use it.

---

## The Padlock Symbol 🔒

You may see a small padlock in the address bar.

This means:

- The connection to the website is encrypted.
- Information you send (like passwords) is protected in transit.

Important:
The padlock does NOT mean the website is trustworthy.
It only means the connection is secure.

Always make sure the website address is spelled correctly.

---

## Try This

1. Open your browser.
2. Click once in the address bar.
3. Type: bbc.co.uk
4. Press Enter.

Now try:
Type: weather in Arrochar

Notice how both work from the same place.

---

## Common Confusion

• The address bar and the search box are often the same thing.  
• You do not need to open Google first to search.  
• If you make a mistake typing, you can simply try again.

---

## Key Takeaway

The **address bar** is where you type website addresses or questions.  
It is the main control centre of your browser.

:::tip Did you know?
1. The **website address in the address bar** can help you spot fake websites.

Scammers sometimes create addresses that look similar to real ones, so it is always worth checking the spelling carefully.

2. **www.bbc.co.uk** and **bbc.co.uk** both take you to the same website.
The “www” part is mostly historical and today it usually makes no difference.
:::
