# Fixing Website Problems

Sometimes websites do not behave as expected.

You might see problems like:

- a page not loading properly
- buttons not working
- a website showing outdated information
- a login page not responding

Before assuming something is seriously wrong, there are a few simple things you can try.

---
## Step 1: Check Your Internet Connection

If a website does not load at all, the problem may be your internet connection.

You might see messages such as:

- "No internet connection"
- "This site can’t be reached"

Things you can try:

1. Check if other websites open.
2. Look for the **Wi-Fi icon** on your device.
3. If possible, try reconnecting to Wi-Fi.

If no websites open, the internet connection may be temporarily unavailable.

:::tip Tip
If several websites are not loading, the problem is usually the internet connection rather than the website itself.
:::

## Step 2: Refresh the Page

Refreshing the page reloads the website.

This often fixes small problems caused by temporary errors.

### How to refresh a page

1. Look for the **refresh button** near the address bar.
2. Click it to reload the page.

The browser will download the page again.

---

## Step 3: Close and Reopen the Page

Sometimes reopening the website can help.

1. Close the tab that is not working.
2. Open a **new tab**.
3. Visit the website again.

This gives the browser a fresh start.

---

## Step 4: Clear Browser Cache

Your browser stores parts of websites on your computer to help them load faster.  
This stored information is called the **cache**.

Occasionally these stored files become outdated and cause problems.

Clearing the cache removes the stored files so the browser downloads fresh versions.

### How to clear the cache

1. Open the **browser menu** (three dots).
2. Select **Settings**.
3. Open **Privacy and security**.
4. Choose **Clear browsing data**.
5. Select **Cached images and files**.
6. Confirm the action.

After clearing the cache, websites may take a little longer to load the first time.

---

:::tip Did you know?
Clearing the cache is one of the most common ways to fix problems with websites.
:::

---

## Things Clearing Cache Does NOT Do

Clearing the cache will **not delete**:

- your bookmarks
- saved passwords (unless you choose to delete them)
- your browser itself

However, some websites may log you out.

---

## Key Takeaway

If a website is not working properly, try these steps:

1. Refresh the page  
2. Open the website again in a new tab  
3. Clear the browser cache  

These simple steps often solve common website problems.
