# 6. Website Cookies

## What Are Cookies?

A **cookie** is a small piece of information that a website stores in your browser.

Websites use cookies to **remember things about your visit**.

For example, cookies can remember:

- Your login details
- Items in your shopping basket
- Your language preference
- Website settings

Cookies help websites work more smoothly and remember your preferences.

---

## Why Do Websites Ask About Cookies?

You may see a **cookie banner** when you visit a website. And often they are annoying.  

This usually asks you to:

- Accept cookies
- Reject cookies
- Manage cookie settings

Websites show these banners because **privacy laws require them to ask permission before storing certain types of cookies**.

---

## Different Types of Cookies

Not all cookies are the same.

### Essential Cookies

These are needed for the website to work properly.

Examples include:

- Logging into accounts
- Saving items in an online shopping basket
- Keeping a website secure

These cookies usually cannot be turned off.

---

### Preference Cookies

These remember your settings.

For example:

- Language selection
- Dark or light mode
- Region settings

These make websites more convenient to use.

---

### Advertising or Tracking Cookies

Some cookies track how people use websites.

They may record:

- Which pages you visit
- How long you stay
- What links you click

This information can be used to show **personalised advertisements**.

---

:::tip Did you know?
The name **cookie** comes from an old programming term called a **"magic cookie"**, which meant a small piece of data passed between programs.
:::

---

## What Should You Click on Cookie Banners?

When a cookie banner appears, you will often see options such as:

- **Accept All**
- **Reject All**
- **Manage Settings**

If you want to reduce tracking, you can usually choose **Reject All** or adjust the settings.

If you accept all cookies, the website may collect more information about how you use it.

:::tip What happens if you close the cookie banner?
If you simply close the cookie banner, the website may still store some cookies.

This depends on how the website is designed.

If you want to choose what cookies are allowed, select **Reject All** or **Manage Settings** instead of closing the banner.
:::

---

## Are Cookies Dangerous?

Cookies themselves are **not viruses** and cannot damage your computer.

They are simply small pieces of data stored by your browser.

However, some cookies are used to **track browsing behaviour**, which is why websites now ask permission.

---

## How Cookies Relate to Private Browsing

When you use **Incognito or Private Mode**:

- Cookies are still created while browsing
- But they are **deleted automatically when the private window is closed**

This is one reason private browsing can help protect privacy on shared devices.

---

## Try This

1. Visit a new website you have not used before.
2. Look for the **cookie banner**.
3. Click **Manage Settings** if available.
4. Explore the options before making a choice.

This helps you understand what you are agreeing to.

---

## Common Confusion

• Cookies are **not viruses**.  
• Cookies cannot see files on your computer.  
• They simply store small pieces of information in your browser.

---

## Key Takeaway

Cookies help websites remember your preferences, but some cookies can also track how websites are used.
