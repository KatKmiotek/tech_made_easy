# Bookmarks and Favorites

## What Are Bookmarks?

A **bookmark** (sometimes called a **favorite**) lets you save a website so you can return to it quickly later.

Instead of searching for the same website every time, you can simply click the bookmark.

Think of it like placing a **bookmark in a book** so you can easily find the page again.

---

## Why Bookmarks Are Useful

Bookmarks help you quickly return to websites you visit often.

For example:

- News websites
- Online banking
- The NHS website
- Email
- Shopping websites

Once a site is bookmarked, you can open it in seconds.

---

## How to Create a Bookmark

Most browsers let you bookmark a page using the **star icon** in the address bar.

Steps:

1. Open the website you want to save.
2. Look for the **⭐ star icon** near the address bar.
3. Click the star.
4. Choose where to save it.
5. Click **Save**.

Your bookmark is now stored in your browser.

---

## Where Do Bookmarks Appear?

Bookmarks can appear in a few places depending on your browser.

Common places include:

- The **Bookmarks Bar** (a row under the address bar)
- The **Bookmarks Menu**
- A **Favorites section** in the browser menu

If you save many bookmarks, they may appear inside folders.

---

## Organizing Bookmarks

Over time you may collect many bookmarks.

To keep things tidy you can:

- Delete bookmarks you no longer use
- Create folders (for example: News, Shopping, Travel)
- Rename bookmarks to something easy to recognize

This makes them easier to find later.

---

## Tablets and iPads – Saving to the Home Screen

Many people using **iPads or tablets** prefer to save websites directly to the **Home Screen**.

This creates an icon that looks like an **app**, making the website very easy to open.

For example, you could place:

- BBC News
- NHS website
- Email
- Your online banking

directly on your device home screen.

---

## How to Add a Website to the Home Screen (iPad / iPhone)

1. Open the website in **Safari**.
2. Tap the **Share button** (square with an arrow pointing up) or settings **...** 3 dots
3. Scroll down and tap **Add to Home Screen**.
4. Choose a name for the icon.
5. Tap **Add**.

The website will now appear on your **Home Screen like an app**.

Just tap it to open the website.

---

## Try This – Create a Bookmarks Folder

Folders help keep your bookmarks organized.

1. Look for the **Bookmarks Bar** below the address bar.
2. **Right-click** on the bookmarks bar.
3. Click **Add Folder** or **New Folder**.
4. Give the folder a name (for example: *News*, *Shopping*, or *Travel*).
5. Press **Save**.

Your new folder will now appear on the bookmarks bar.

---

## Try This – Save a Bookmark

1. Open your browser.
2. Go to a website you like (for example: **bbc.co.uk**).
3. Click the **⭐ star icon** in the address bar.
4. In the name box, type a simple name (for example: **News**).
5. In the folder dropdown select the folder you created.
6. Click **Save**.

Your bookmark is now stored in your browser.

Try opening it again using your bookmarks list.

---

## Common Confusion

• Bookmarks are saved **inside your browser**, not on the website itself.  
• If you change devices, your bookmarks may not appear unless your browser is **synced**.  
• Bookmarks simply save the **link** to a website.

---

:::tip Did you know?
The word **bookmark** comes from the paper markers people place inside books to remember a page.

Internet browsers borrowed the same idea for saving websites.
:::

---

## Key Takeaway

Bookmarks (or favorites) let you **save websites so you can open them quickly without searching again**.

On tablets and iPads you can also **save websites directly to your home screen**, making them behave like apps.
